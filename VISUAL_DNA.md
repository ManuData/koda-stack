## Color Behaviour

> How colors are used, not just what they are. (See `CLAUDE.md` for hex values.)
> References: `assets/moodboard/color/color-01-mountain-sunrise.png`, `color-02-beach-sunset.png`, `color-03-seascape-dusk.png`

- **Dominant tone**: Warm golden-orange at the source of light, transitioning to deep purple-blue in shadows and upper sky
- **Background approach**: Almost always gradient — never flat colour. Dark rich base (near-black indigo) grading into warm or cool midtones
- **Gradient usage**: Directional from a single light source (horizon, sun). Warm-to-cool diagonal: gold/orange → pink/magenta → deep blue/purple
- **Accent placement**: The brightest warm tone (yellow-gold or orange) appears at a single focal point — the rest of the frame earns its drama from contrast
- **Colour temperature split**: Lower third warm (golden, orange, flamingo), upper two-thirds cool (cobalt, lavender, nebula purple)
- **Silhouette role**: Dark purple-grey silhouettes (`~#2D2535–#3A3550`) create depth layers between warm light source and cool sky
- **What's always absent**: Flat, even lighting. Artificial-looking colours. Neon or highly saturated single hues without gradation.

## Graphic Language — Connecting Lines

> The signature brand graphic element. Store assets in `assets/moodboard/branding/`.
> References: `branding-01-connecting-line-buyer-persona-sea-summer.png`, `branding-02-connecting-line-buyer-persona-woman-travel.png`, `branding-03-connecting-line-buyer-persona-vibes.png`, `branding-04-connecting-line-device.jpg`, `branding-05-connecting-line-buyer-persona-fun.png`, `branding-06-connecting-line-buyer-persona-women-young.png`

- **Origin**: Derived from the wave/curve forms embedded in the Avelsky logo
- **Name**: Connecting Lines (official brand name)
- **Meaning**: Represents constant companionship through the journey, the idea of flow, and the freedom to enjoy every moment without limits
- **Form**: Loose, fluid loop — an open infinity-like curve, not a closed shape. Always in motion, never rigid
- **Colour states**:
  - Solid yellow (`#F5E264`) on bright photography
  - Gradient (flamingo pink → golden orange → sunrise yellow) on dark/purple backgrounds
- **Usage**: Overlaid on top of photography and video. Positioned off-centre, partially bleeds off frame
- **Scale**: Always large relative to the frame — never a small decorative touch
- **What it is NOT**: A border, an underline, a divider. It is a graphic companion, not a structural element

## What Makes This Brand Visually Distinct

> The 5 things that, if you removed them, would make this look like any other brand.
> References: `branding-01` through `branding-06` in `assets/moodboard/branding/`

1. **The Connecting Line** — the signature curved graphic derived from the logo's wave form. Yellow on bright content, gradient on dark. Remove it and the brand loses its face.
2. **Vibrant purple as a full-bleed stage** — `#7B2FD8` isn't used as an accent here; it's the entire background. It frames everything. Most brands use purple sparingly. This brand owns it as architecture.
3. **Real people in unguarded, joyful moments** — not posed talent, not stock. Candid energy (woman upside down, mid-laugh). The humanity is the content, not the backdrop for it.
4. **Edge-to-edge typography that bleeds off frame** — "Connecting" and "moments" are so large they become compositional elements, not just headlines. The text is structural.
5. **Gradient graphics that echo the brand colour system** — the Connecting Line's gradient (pink → orange → gold) is the brand palette in motion, tying photography to identity without heavy-handed treatment.
