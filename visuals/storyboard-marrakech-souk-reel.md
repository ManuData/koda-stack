## Motion Direction — Shot by Shot

**Shot 1 — G (Hook) — 1.5s**
Anchor: center frame on the white figure.
Motion: slow zoom in 1.0× → 1.12×. The white figure enlarges slightly — the eye locks on her before it reads the spices around her.
Start: full frame. End: slightly tighter on center.

**Shot 2 — B (World) — 2.0s**
Anchor: full souk corridor.
Motion: slow pan right 0% → 3% of frame width. Follows the natural direction of her movement and the golden light source.
Start: left edge of frame has warm glow. End: glow centered.

**Shot 3 — I (Emotional Peak) — 2.5s** ← longest shot, hold it
Anchor: the glowing amber stall she faces.
Motion: very slow push in 1.0× → 1.08×. Pulls the viewer INTO the glow she's drawn to. Almost imperceptible — the effect is felt not seen.
Start: her full silhouette with stall behind. End: stall fills slightly more frame.

**Shot 4 — E (Immersion) — 2.0s**
Anchor: lanterns upper-left cluster.
Motion: slow zoom in 1.0× → 1.15× focused upper-left. The lanterns bloom into focus as the spices drop slightly out of frame.
Start: full stall wide. End: lanterns dominate.

**Shot 5 — A (Energy) — 1.5s**
Anchor: her extended arm + kicked-back leg.
Motion: slow zoom OUT 1.1× → 1.0×. Counter to previous zoom-ins — a visual breath out. Releases tension.
Start: slightly tight on her. End: full figure with souk corridor.

**Shot 6 — C (Rhythm Break) — 2.0s**
Anchor: spice stripe pattern bottom-right.
Motion: slow tilt DOWN 0% → 2.5% of frame height. Reveals more of the graphic spice pattern below her. The overhead becomes even more abstract.
Start: equal balance between her and spices. End: spice stripes more dominant.

**Shot 7 — K (Close) — 3.5s**
Anchor: her figure in the corridor.
Motion: very slow push in 1.0× → 1.1×. The corridor compresses softly around her. She fills more of the frame by the end without a cut. Let it breathe the full 3.5 seconds.
Start: full wide corridor. End: she is the frame.

## Sound Design Brief

> Hand this to your sound editor. No music. No voiceover. Pure location audio.

```
SOUND DESIGN
---
Approach: Cinematic ambient soundscape — Marrakech medina, interior souk.
          Build from near-silent to full immersion, then exhale at the end.
          All sounds must feel sourced from the scene, not added.

Layer 1 — BED (continuous, full reel):
  Distant medina ambient hum. Low, warm. Market life heard from one
  street over — not in your face. Starts at 15% volume, rises to 40%
  by shot 6, settles to 30% on shot 7.

Layer 2 — TEXTURE (shots 1–2, 0:00–0:03.5):
  Fabric rustle. A heavy textile brushed by a hand or a light breeze.
  Single natural sound event, not looped. Fades out by shot 3.

Layer 3 — TACTILE (shots 3–4, 0:03.5–0:08.0):
  Spice being touched or scooped — a soft dry grain sound. Close mic.
  Intimate. This is the sound of her hand in the spice tray.
  Layered under: distant vendor call in Darija Arabic — one phrase,
  not translated, not subtitled. Heard, not understood.

Layer 4 — FOOTSTEP (shot 5, 0:08.0–0:09.5):
  Single cobblestone footstep — sandal on stone. Sharp, clean, one beat.
  Punctuates the energy shot. Synced to the hard cut in.

Layer 5 — SWELL (shot 6, 0:09.5–0:11.5):
  Market ambient rises slightly — as if stepping deeper in.
  Layered murmur of voices, a coin sound, a distant hammering — all
  Marrakech souk signatures. 3–4 sounds blended, none dominant.

Layer 6 — EXHALE (shot 7, 0:11.5–0:15.0):
  All layers blend and soften. The market breathes around her.
  Fade out: start fade at 0:14.0, reach silence at 0:15.0.
  No abrupt cut on the audio — the image cuts to black, the sound
  fades 1 second after. The medina hum is the last thing heard.

NEVER: music bed, VO, synthetic sounds, reverb that doesn't match
  the space, sudden silence, sound effects that feel "added."
---
```

## Editor Notes

- **Color grade direction**: warm, rich, slightly desaturated midtones. The amber-orange-gold palette is the story. Don't cool it down. Reference: Kodak Portra 400 pushed warm.
- **Aspect ratio**: 9:16 — all images are portrait or need to be cropped from the source files. Center-crop from the 3:4 originals, losing some top/bottom.
- **No text overlays** — the brief is pure image and sound. If a brand end card is needed, add 1.5s black frame after shot 7 with brand mark only (white on black, centered, no animation).
- **Export**: H.264, 1080×1920, 30fps minimum.
- **Tools**: Final Cut Pro / Premiere / CapCut all viable. Ken Burns motion can be keyframed in any of these.
