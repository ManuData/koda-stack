# ART DIRECTION — Instagram Ad: The Morning the City is Yours (Concept B)
> Skill: `/concept` | Format: 3:4 | Platform: Instagram Feed

---

## Concept Brief

A single editorial hero image for a luxury Instagram ad. No text. No logo. No CTA. No graphic overlays. Pure photography. A luxury loft interior at pre-dawn — warm amber light inside contrasting against the cool blue of New York just waking up. A figure in silhouette at floor-to-ceiling windows, the city suspended and still beyond the glass. Two temperatures, two worlds, one pane of glass between them.

---

## Art Direction

```
ART DIRECTION
---
Palette:
  #211F54 — Deep Space Indigo (pre-dawn sky, upper exterior)
  #FFB067 — Golden Orange (interior warm light, lamp glow, dawn rim)
  #FFBFC0 — Flamingo Pink (interior warm transition, soft lamp scatter)
  #8DC8F5 — Vibrant Sky Blue (pre-dawn exterior sky, cool city tone)
  #C5AAFF — Lavender Mist (sky transition, exterior cool shadow)

Mood: The private New York that exists before the city remembers it's New York.
      Warm inside, cool blue outside. Two stills meeting at the glass.
      Interior as sanctuary, city as spectacle — both yours, simultaneously.

Lighting:
  INTERIOR: Single warm light source — a floor lamp or early dawn light creeping in.
  Warm amber (#FFB067) and flamingo pink (#FFBFC0) dominate the interior.
  Soft, directional — not flat or overhead. Creates long warm shadows on the floor.
  EXTERIOR: Pre-dawn cool blue — city lights still on, sky a deep blue-grey transitioning
  to indigo (#211F54) at the top of the frame. Some building windows glow warm amber.
  The colour temperature split is extreme and deliberate: WARM inside / COOL outside.
  The window glass creates a clean threshold between the two worlds.
  No flash. No artificial studio light. Interior lamp + dawn exterior only.

Composition:
  3:4 portrait (1080×1440).
  Wide minimal loft interior — the room breathes, not cluttered.
  Floor-to-ceiling glass wall occupies most of the frame's far end.
  Figure in silhouette stands or sits at the window — small, seen from behind or side,
  occupying no more than 20% of frame width. Suggestion of a person, not a portrait.
  Coffee cup or hand detail optional — keeps it intimate and human.
  The window frame creates a natural grid that echoes the city grid beyond.
  Interior foreground: warm, textured — pale oak floor, low furniture edge, soft shadows.
  Exterior through glass: vast, cool, the Manhattan skyline still and glowing.
  Negative space dominates — this is a frame about atmosphere, not objects.

Environment:
  Tribeca, SoHo, or West Village luxury loft — minimal, considered, expensive-feeling.
  Pale oak or concrete floors. Low, warm furniture. A reading chair or sofa partially visible.
  No clutter, no decoration, no visible brand objects.
  Exterior: Manhattan pre-dawn skyline — mix of residential and commercial towers,
  some windows glowing amber, the street grid visible below in cool blue light.
  No Times Square signage, no Statue of Liberty, no yellow cabs visible.
  Sky: deep indigo-blue at top, lightening slightly toward the horizon where dawn is approaching.

Texture:
  Interior: warm, soft — wood grain on floor, fabric on furniture edge.
  Glass: clean, reflective — subtle reflection of the warm interior in the pane.
  Exterior: cool, crisp — building facades, city grid, pre-dawn sky.
  Ultra clean luxury editorial quality. No film grain. No heavy processing.

NO TEXT, NO GRAPHICS:
  No headline. No CTA. No logo. No caption. No watermark. No graphic overlay of any kind.
  Pure editorial photography only.

References:
  1. Edward Hopper — interior light and urban solitude, warmth inside looking out
  2. Sofia Coppola, Lost in Translation — hotel room frames, quiet urban intimacy
  3. VISUAL_DNA.md — color-03-seascape-dusk.png (full warm-cool palette contrast)

Do NOT:
  - Any text, type, headline, logo, watermark, caption or graphic overlay
  - Times Square, Statue of Liberty, yellow cabs, tourist landmarks
  - Busy or cluttered interior — keep it minimal and luxurious
  - Even or flat interior lighting — warmth must be directional and atmospheric
  - Figure larger than 20% of frame — keep them small at the window
  - Daytime exterior — must be pre-dawn, city lights still on
  - Film grain, heavy processing, or oversaturated colours
  - Modern tech clutter — no screens, no devices visible
  - Cold or clinical interior — warmth is essential
```
