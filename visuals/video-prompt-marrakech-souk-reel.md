## Master Video Prompt — Full Reel (15 seconds)

> Use this as the overarching direction brief for the full sequence.
> For AI video tools that generate shot-by-shot (4–10s clips), use the
> individual shot prompts below and stitch in post.

```
MASTER PROMPT
---
A 15-second cinematic reel shot entirely in a Marrakech medina souk,
Morocco. The reel opens from a dead overhead 90-degree bird's-eye
perspective looking straight down onto a cobblestone alley, a solo
woman in white wide-leg linen trousers and white top with an orange
leather crossbody bag is the only light element against the dark
cobblestone floor, surrounded by colorful spice trays and craft
barrels. The camera begins a slow, almost imperceptible vertical
descent — dropping from the overhead position toward eye level over
the course of the first 3 seconds, as if falling gently into the
scene. No jump cuts in the first 3 seconds — the descent is fluid
and continuous. As the camera reaches eye level at the 3-second mark,
the scene transitions to a series of intimate eye-level perspectives:
the woman moving through the souk corridor, reaching for spice trays,
absorbed by the glowing amber light of a market stall. The atmosphere
is warm, sensory, unhurried. The subject never looks at the camera —
she is absorbed in the space. The final frame holds on her from behind,
facing a glowing amber stall, completely still. Pure ambient sound —
medina hum, spice grain textures, fabric rustle, distant Darija voices,
cobblestone footsteps. No music. No voiceover. Kodak Portra 400 warm
film grain throughout. Natural rich amber and golden lighting.
```

### SHOT 1 — G (Hook) | 1.5s | Overhead descent
```
INPUT IMAGE: Shot G (overhead bird's eye)
TARGET DURATION: 2s (trim to 1.5s in edit)

PROMPT:
Starting from a perfect 90-degree overhead bird's-eye view looking
straight down onto a Marrakech medina souk cobblestone floor, the
camera performs an extremely slow, smooth vertical descent — as if
gently falling from directly above, dropping approximately 30% closer
to the subject over 2 seconds. The woman in white below remains
centered in the frame throughout. Her white outfit is the anchor point
as the dark cobblestones and surrounding colorful spice barrels fill
the frame. No horizontal movement. No rotation. Pure vertical descent,
smooth as a crane shot. The subject's arm is slightly extended toward
the spice display. Very subtle life in the scene — a barely perceptible
sway of a hanging fabric at the upper frame edge. Warm amber and golden
light from the market stalls. Kodak Portra 400 film grain. Rich warm
color palette. Cinematic, not documentary. The descent should feel like
gravity made graceful.

CAMERA: Straight down → slow vertical descent Z-axis
MOTION SPEED: Extremely slow — imperceptible in real time, felt not seen
SUBJECT MOTION: Minimal — very slight weight shift, arm extended
ATMOSPHERE: Warm, still, graphic
```

## Technical Specs — All Shots

```
ASPECT RATIO:    9:16 vertical (crop source 3:4 images — center crop,
                 lose top/bottom, keep subject centered)
RESOLUTION:      1080 × 1920 minimum
FRAME RATE:      24fps — cinematic, not 30fps
GRAIN:           Kodak Portra 400 film grain overlay at 15–20% opacity
                 across all shots — applied in post if not in generation
COLOR GRADE:     Warm. Amber dominant. Slightly desaturated midtones.
                 Shadows: warm brown-indigo, never cold grey.
                 Highlights: amber-gold, not blown.
                 Reference: Kodak Portra pushed 1 stop warm.
TRANSITIONS:     Hard cuts only between all shots — no dissolves, no fades.
                 Exception: final shot fades to black over last 0.5s.
MOTION:          All camera moves at 10–20% of what feels "normal speed."
                 If it looks like camera movement, it's too fast.
                 The motion should only be felt in retrospect.
```
